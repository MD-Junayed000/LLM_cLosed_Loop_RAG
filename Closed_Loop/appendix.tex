\section*{Appendix A. Reproducibility and Execution Notes}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Appendix A. Reproducibility and Execution Notes}

\subsection*{A.1 Execution Environment}

All backbone-specific experiments were executed on Google Colab using an NVIDIA A100 GPU (40\,GB, FP16). The language-model backbones were kept frozen throughout; only the lightweight hallucination probes were trained. The comparison and visualization notebook loaded exported metrics and generated cross-model tables and figures.

The final experimental pipeline:
\[
\begin{aligned}
\text{Frozen LLM} 
&\to \text{CEV/IAV extraction} 
\to \text{Probe training} 
\to \text{Calibration} \\
&\to \text{Fusion} 
\to \text{Controller}
\end{aligned}
\]

\section*{Appendix C. Degeneration and Artifact Handling}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Appendix C. Degeneration and Artifact Handling}

\subsection*{C.1 Repetition Guard}

A 4-gram repetition ratio is used to detect degenerate generation loops:
\begin{equation*}
    r_{\text{4-gram}} = 1 - \frac{\text{\# unique 4-grams}}{\text{\# total 4-grams}}
\end{equation*}
A higher value indicates more repetition. If exceeded, the output is routed toward regeneration or abstention.

\section*{Appendix D. Unused Learned Policy Component}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Appendix D. Unused Learned Policy Component}

\subsection*{D.1 PolicyNetwork Design}

An optional learned policy controller was explored but not retained. The proposed policy state was:
\[
    \mathbf{s} = (p_{\text{fused}},\; p_{\text{CEV}},\; p_{\text{IAV}},\; q_{\text{ret}},\; a / N_{\max})
\]
The learned policy produced a softmax distribution over \{\textsc{Regenerate}, \textsc{Re-Retrieve}, \textsc{Abstain}\}. The \textsc{Accept} decision remained threshold-based.

\subsection*{D.2 REINFORCE Exploration}

\section*{Appendix E. Deterministic Controller Pseudocode}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Appendix E. Deterministic Controller Pseudocode}

\begin{pseudocodebox}[Deterministic Controller --- Final System]
\begin{tabbing}
\hspace{0.8cm}\=\hspace{3.6cm}\=\kill
\textbf{Input:}\\
\> $p_{\text{fused}}$ \> calibrated hallucination-risk probability\\
\> $q_{\text{ret}}$ \> retrieval-quality score\\
\> $\text{attempts}$ \> current retry count\\
\> $\theta_{\text{hallu}}$ \> intervention threshold\\
\> $\theta_{\text{abs}}$ \> abstention threshold\\
\> $\theta_{\text{ret}}$ \> retrieval-quality threshold\\
\> $\text{max\_retries}$ \> maximum allowed attempts\\[8pt]
\textbf{Algorithm:}\\
\> \textbf{if} attempts $\geq$ max\_retries:\\
\>\> \textbf{return} \textcolor{abstainred}{ABSTAIN}\\[3pt]
\> \textbf{if} $p_{\text{fused}} < \theta_{\text{hallu}}$:\\
\>\> \textbf{return} \textcolor{acceptgreen}{ACCEPT}\\[3pt]
\> \textbf{if} $p_{\text{fused}} \geq \theta_{\text{abs}}$:\\
\>\> \textbf{return} \textcolor{abstainred}{ABSTAIN}\\[3pt]
\> \textbf{if} $q_{\text{ret}} < \theta_{\text{ret}}$:\\
\>\> \textbf{return} \textcolor{regenblue}{RE-RETRIEVE}\\[3pt]
\> \textbf{return} \textcolor{regenblue}{REGENERATE}
\end{tabbing}
\end{pseudocodebox}

\vspace{0.5em}
\noindent It is possible to understand and check the deterministic controller, and it is perfectly in line with the closed-loop RAG action space.
