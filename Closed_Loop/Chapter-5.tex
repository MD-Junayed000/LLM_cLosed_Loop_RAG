\section{Overview of the Closed-Loop RAG Pipeline}
\label{sec:pipeline_overview}

Chapter~\ref{ch:background} defined the two internal activation signals used in this work: the \textbf{Contextualized Embedding Vector (CEV)} from the residual stream and the \textbf{Intermediate Activation Vector (IAV)} from the SwiGLU feed-forward sublayer. This section describes how these signals are integrated into a complete closed-loop RAG system.

\section{Core Functional Blocks}
\label{sec:core_blocks}

The system consists of four main functional blocks.

\subsection{Retrieval Engine}
\label{subsec:retrieval_engine}

The retrieval engine follows a standard dense-retrieval RAG design. Documents are encoded using the BAAI/bge-base-en-v1.5 embedding model~\cite{xiao2023bge}, producing 768-dimensional dense vectors. The document embeddings are indexed using FAISS~\cite{johnson2019faiss}. At inference time, the user query is encoded by the same embedding model, and the top-$k$ passages are retrieved.

The retrieval quality score is defined as the mean similarity between the query and the top-$k$ retrieved passages:
\begin{equation}
    q_{\text{ret}} = \frac{1}{k} \sum_{i=1}^{k} \text{sim}(\mathbf{q},\, \mathbf{d}_i)
    \label{eq:qret}
\end{equation}
where $\mathbf{q}$ is the query embedding and $\mathbf{d}_i$ is the $i$-th retrieved passage embedding. This score is used as a proxy for answerability and retrieval relevance.

\subsection{LLM Backbone with Internal Hooks}
\label{subsec:llm_backbone}

The retrieved context and user query are passed to a frozen decoder-only LLM backbone. This work evaluates the same architecture across three backbones:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.2
    \item LLaMA-3-8B-Instruct
    \item Qwen3-8B
\end{itemize}

Forward hooks are registered at a single mid-depth decoder layer at approximately $L/2$. At that layer, the system captures $\mathbf{z}_{\text{CEV}}^{(\ell^*)}$ from the residual-stream output, and $\mathbf{z}_{\text{IAV}}^{(\ell^*)}$ from the SwiGLU FFN intermediate activation before $W_{\text{down}}$. Section~\ref{sec:layer_selection} reports the ablation that motivates this single-layer choice.

\subsection{Hallucination Detection Network}
\label{subsec:detection_network}

Two independent MLP probes are trained:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{CEV probe}: receives the CEV feature vector $\boldsymbol{\phi}(\mathbf{x})$.
    \item \textbf{IAV probe}: receives the IAV feature vector $\boldsymbol{\psi}(\mathbf{x})$.
\end{enumerate}

The probes output two hallucination probabilities:
\begin{align}
    p_{\text{CEV}} &= P(y = 1 \mid \boldsymbol{\phi}(\mathbf{x})) \label{eq:pcev}\\[4pt]
    p_{\text{IAV}} &= P(y = 1 \mid \boldsymbol{\psi}(\mathbf{x})) \label{eq:piav}
\end{align}
where $y = 1$ denotes hallucination. The two probabilities are fused using a validation-tuned convex combination:
\begin{equation}
    \boxed{\;p_{\text{fused}} = w \cdot p_{\text{CEV}} + (1 - w) \cdot p_{\text{IAV}}\;}
    \label{eq:pfused}
\end{equation}
where $w$ is selected on the validation split. In these single-layer runs, the tuned weights are: $0.50/0.50$ for Mistral, $0.60/0.40$ (CEV-leaning) for Qwen, and $0.45/0.55$ (slightly IAV-leaning) for LLaMA---confirming that both signals contribute and neither dominates universally.

\subsection{Closed-Loop Policy Controller}
\label{subsec:controller_overview}

The controller consumes $p_{\text{fused}}$, the retrieval-quality score $q_{\text{ret}}$, and the retry count, and selects one of four actions. The full decision rule and thresholds are given in Section~\ref{sec:controller_logic}.

\section{Internal-State Feature Extraction}
\label{sec:feature_extraction}

Let $L$ denote the total number of decoder layers. The system selects a single mid-depth hook layer:
\begin{equation}
    \ell^* = \lfloor L/2 \rfloor
    \label{eq:layer_selection}
\end{equation}

At the selected layer $\ell^*$, the CEV is extracted from the block output:
\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{z}_{\text{CEV}}^{(\ell^*)} = \mathbf{h}_{\text{out}}^{(\ell^*)}
    \label{eq:cev_extract}
\end{equation}

The IAV is extracted from the SwiGLU FFN intermediate activation:
\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{z}_{\text{IAV}}^{(\ell^*)} = \text{SiLU}\bigl(W_{\text{gate}}^{(\ell^*)} \text{RMSNorm}(\mathbf{h}_{\ell^*})\bigr) \odot \bigl(W_{\text{up}}^{(\ell^*)} \text{RMSNorm}(\mathbf{h}_{\ell^*})\bigr)
    \label{eq:iav_extract}
\end{equation}

For autoregressive decoding, the \emph{final token} representation is used because it has attended to the full preceding context:
\begin{align}
    \boldsymbol{\phi}(\mathbf{x}) &= \mathbf{z}_{\text{CEV,last}}^{(\ell^*)} \label{eq:phi}\\[3pt]
    \boldsymbol{\psi}(\mathbf{x}) &= \mathbf{z}_{\text{IAV,last}}^{(\ell^*)} \label{eq:psi}
\end{align}

\label{sec:layer_selection}

To decide whether that extra width earns its cost, each backbone was put through a controlled layer-wise ablation.

\section{Probe Architecture and Calibration}
\label{sec:probe_arch}

Each hallucination probe is a lightweight two-hidden-layer MLP. Separate probes are used for CEV and IAV because the two feature types live in different vector spaces.

The probe architecture is:
\[
    \text{Input} \to \text{Linear}(d, 256) \to \text{BN} \to \text{ReLU} \to \text{Dropout} \to \text{Linear}(256, 128) \to \text{BN} \to \text{ReLU} \to \text{Dropout} \to \text{Linear}(128, 2)
\]

The output logits are temperature-scaled before softmax calibration:
\begin{equation}
    P(y = 1) = \text{softmax}\!\left(\frac{\mathbf{z}}{T}\right)
    \label{eq:temp_scaling}
\end{equation}
where $T$ is selected on the validation split to minimize negative log-likelihood.

\section{Training and Fusion Summary}
\label{sec:training_fusion_summary}

The probes are trained on labeled RAG hallucination examples using class-weighted cross-entropy loss with label smoothing. AdamW is used for optimization~\cite{loshchilov2019adamw,kingma2015adam}, and temperature scaling is applied for calibration~\cite{guo2017calibration}. The CEV and IAV probes are trained independently and fused after calibration using the validation-tuned convex combination of Eq.~\eqref{eq:pfused}.

The complete training and fusion settings are consolidated in Table~\ref{tab:training_config} (Section~\ref{sec:training_protocol}).
